# Full disclosure

I think people deserve context, so here is mine.

I am not a programmer. I am a ceramic artist. I have no funding, no investors, no real technical expertise. I have ideas, some time, and just enough stubbornness to sit down and try.

If you look through my GitHub projects, my ethos becomes fairly clear. I make open source software because I believe it is one of the last genuinely healthy corners of the internet. A kind of resistance, if you will. A place where people still build things to share them, not to extract value.

And I will be honest: these apps would not exist without AI, local whenever possible. That creates real human friction in me. I know these systems were built on the invisible labour of millions of people who were never asked, and on data centers run by big, ethically questionable corporations.

None of that sits easily with me. But I cannot ignore what these tools now make possible for someone like me. Without them, I simply could not build these things.

So I try to find the good in it. If this cacophony of data already exists, then let what I build with it go back to the community. Free, open, no subscriptions, no data collection. Forever.

Built on the shoulders of thousands of engineers, programmers and researchers who came before. What belongs to the people should return to the people.

Let's not burn the data centers. Let's make them ours.
